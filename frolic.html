turn a crowd into teams — before the crowd walks away.

frolic wanted visibility: live gaming leagues in colleges and public places. the model was simple — a captain creates a team, players join under them, every score counts, and the top of the leaderboard takes the reward. the hard part was the first thirty seconds: getting a stranger in a crowd into a team before they lose interest.

the problem

at a live event, signup friction is death. forms, app-store hunting, passcodes read out loud over a crowd — every step loses people.

my answer, back in 2023: put a qr where the crowd already is. scan it, land in the league, pick your team. the flow in five steps:

  1. the captain creates the league group — one accountable person per team.
  2. the qr goes up at the venue — poster, standee, a phone held up in a hostel corridor.
  3. players scan and land under the captain — passcode as fallback, group name and they're in.
  4. everyone plays, every score adds — individual effort, team total.
  5. the leaderboard settles it — top team takes the reward.
decisions underneath

  1. qr kills the queue

    the join moment happens where the crowd stands, not where a form lives. scan beats type, every time, in every corridor.

  2. captain owns the huddle

    public places are chaos. one accountable person per team — creating, inviting, starting — turns chaos into a roster.

  3. team score, not solo score

    every person's points feed the team total, so nobody is a spectator. the reward at the top keeps the whole board playing.

what it taught me

this was my first big system at reliance fynd, and where i learned to trade opinion for evidence — usability testing across frolic and fan11 surfaced 20+ friction points that engineering and i fixed together. the leaderboard's v-1 → v-2 on this very board is that habit, visible.

a league in a public place is just onboarding with an audience. win the first thirty seconds and the rest plays itself.
